V1


V1 is a Supreme Machine and the protagonist and player character of ULTRAKILL. V1 is the first version of V model, an earlier prototype that came before V2. It specializes in quick repairs, using an experimental armor plating that can absorb blood on contact rather than a separate blood refueling process.

Piercer Revolver


The Piercer Revolver is the first gun found in ULTRAKILL and is the blue variant of the Revolver, it can deal moderate amounts of damage with its primary fire and can pierce through multiple targets with its secondary fire.


Marksman Revolver


The Marksman Revolver appears almost identical to the Piercer Revolver, but has neon green lights as well as a different interface that indicates the amount of available coins. The primary fire is identical to the Piercer revolver, but the secondary fire makes V1 toss a coin into the air, which when shot at, hitscans projectiles will bereflected onto enemies weapoints


Alternate Revolver



The alternate Revolvers can be unlocked after interacting with all four secret slabs found all throughout Limbo. It dies significantly more damage than the default variants of the revolvers, but it has a slower fire-rate.

Core Eject Shotgun


The Core Eject Shotgun appears to be a futuristic double-barrel break-action shotgun. 2 cylindrical cores can be found in the chamber, which is flipped open to vent the overheated cores on primary fire and ejected as a single projectile on secondary fire, which turns the cores into an explosive projectile. After firing a core, the 2 cores will be discarded and replaced.


Pump Charge Shotgun


The Pump Charge variant of the shotgun functions very similarly to the Core Eject variant, the only difference being that you can "pump" the shotgun 2 times for additional pelletes, however, if you pump 3 times, it causes the gun to "overpump", which makes the shotgun explode when fired, causing a lot of damage to enemies, but also to the player

Attractor Nailgun


The Attractor Nailgun is a bulky, double-barreled weapon that resembles a minigun. Holding primary fire will cause the Attractor Nailgun to start firing nails at a rapid rate, and pressing alt fire will launch an nail magnet that attracts the fired nails. Centered on the weapon is a neon blue interface that indicates ammo count and the amount of available nail magnets.


Overheat Nailgun


The Overheat Nailgun functions very similarly to the Attractor variant, the difference being it can fire indefinetly, at the cost of slowly reducing it's fire rate and chargin one of the batteries, however, the secondary fire causes the nailgun to "overheat", consuming one of the battery charges and spewing tons of fiery nails.

Charged Electric Railcannon


The Electric Railcannon is a large blue and silver gun that, as its name suggests, resembles a hybrid between a railgun and a cannon. Pressing primary fire will instantly fire a piercing hitscan beam, which can pierce through an infinite amount of enemies and deal 8 damage on each hit .


Charged Screwdriver Railcannon


The Screwdriver railcannon functions very differently than it's default variant, instead of a burst damage piercing hitscan beam, it fires a high-velocity screwdriver that sticks into enemies, and constantly damages them for 8 seconds, providing and infinite source of healing and doing 1 damage for 8 seconds on a single target.

Malicious Railcannon


The Malicious Railcannon, as it's name suggests, functions very diffirently than the default variant, it launches an hitscan beam that will produce a large explosion on impact, nearly identical to the Malicious Face enemy, being very useful for clearing enemy hordes.

Feedbacker


The feedbacker is v1's default arm, The key feature of the Feedbacker is its ability to parry. With precise timing, punching almost any projectile in the game will boost its speed, and send it flying in the direction of the player's crosshair. In addition, physically punching an enemy during their parry window will cause a parry punch, punching them for 5 damage and (usually) cancelling their attack.


KnuckleBlaster


The KnuckleBlaster is an alternative to V1's default arm, punching with this arm will deal heavy damage and send enemies flying away, but cannot parry.

Whiplash


Unlike the Feedbacker or Knuckleblaster, you cannot switch to the Whiplash; instead, it is automatically used by pressing its dedicated hotkey (R by default), which will pull you to big enemies and grapple points and bring small enemies to you. You can also dash while in the process of pulling/being pulled by an object.
